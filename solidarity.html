November 2011

Solidarity with Ukraine

Location: Maidan Nezalezhnosti, Kyiv, Ukraine

Estimated Creation Date: November 2022

Description: Behind a metal tank trap, on a stone barrier, two girls are painted. The tank trap creates the illusion that the two girls are playing on a seesaw.

Theme:Defiance/resistance, strength in hardship, moral questions about war.

Context: February 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine after decades of conflict between the two nations. These works support Ukraine’s response to Russian forces.

Analysis: Like with many of his works, in Solidarity with Ukraine, Banksy chooses to depict children in a woeful context. As Ukraine is currently under attack, none of its inhabitants are safe. A lack of security often leads people to hide and isolate themselves in the interest of preserving whatever safety they can. However, each piece in the Solidarity with Ukraine series symbolizes defiance; children fight back either literally or figuratively. Literally, there are depictions of children throwing men on the ground—all the more impactful for Banksy’s juxtaposition of size in the figures. Figuratively, there are children playing on war paraphernalia—a protest of control. By portraying the children as playful, Banksy asserts that Ukraine will not give in to Russian invasion. Ukraine maintains its autonomy, even under extreme duress.
