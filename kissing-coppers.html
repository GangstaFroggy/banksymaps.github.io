Kissing Coppers

Location: The Prince Albert pub in Brighton

Estimated Creation Date: 2004

Description: Two policement in uniform kissing

Theme: LGBTQ+ protest

Context: The piece was unveiled in 2004, when gay marriage had not been legalized in the UK. The piece was unveiled on the wall of The Prince Albert Pub in Brighton in 2004. Brighton is considered the LGBTQ+ capital of the UK. Brighton’s history with the LGBTQ+ community dates back all the way to the Napoleonic Wars in 1803, and currently Brighton also hosts the largest UK LGBTQ+ celebration each year.

Analysis: As Banksy did not provide his thought process, it cannot be determined with absolute certainty what his intentions were when creating this piece. Either Banksy was advocating for greater acceptance of homosexuality or ridiculing the authority of policemen. However, given Banksy’s tendencies towards more liberal topics and the lack of any obviously homophobic works, it is a popular belief that this work leans more towards normalizing homosexuality. If officers of the law—who represent the law—are allowed to be homosexual, then the rest of the population is also permitted to be queer.
