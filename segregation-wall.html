March 2017

The Segregation Wall

Location: West Bank Wall, West Bank, Palestine

Estimated Creation Date: 2005

Description: 7 murals. One depicts a hole. Through the faux hole in the wall, a child stands.p>

Theme: Imprisonment, oppression.

Context: The wall was built in 2002, by the Israeli government. It is controlled by a series of checkpoints and runs for about 700 kilometers.

Analysis: In a rare vocalization, Banksy makes his feelings about this piece explicit. Banksy states the wall “essentially turns Palestine into the world’s largest open prison.” For a while, Banksy's website held a reminder that the barrier is considered illegal by the United Nations. The overall message is clear: Banksy is denouncing the conflict between Israel and Palestine. In typical Banksy style, children portray innocence and are represented in an attempt to convince the audience of the moral turpitude of the situation.
