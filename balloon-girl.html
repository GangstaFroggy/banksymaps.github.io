Girl With Balloon

Location: Waterloo Bridge, South Bank, London, England (piece is no longer there)

Estimated Creation Date: 2002

Description: A young girl drawn in black and white stands with her right arm outstretched towards a red, heart-shaped balloon. Often paired with the phrase “There is always hope.”

Theme: The fragility of innocence and hope.

Context: After the initial artwork was made, several later iterations addressed various social campaigns, including the Syrian refugee crisis and the 2017 UK election. Additionally, in 2018, a framed copy was sold, but the frame shredded the work as part of the experience, dubbing it “Love is in the Bin.”

Analysis: This piece is perhaps the most famous of Banksy’s works and demonstrates some of his typical stylistic features. Often, Banksy will use children to symbolize innocence and highlight the conditions that people face. Images using children are typically more controversial; especially in present society, children are meant to be protected. However, Banksy’s use of children in tragic scenarios emphasizes their laments, bringing their struggles to the viewer’s attention more effectively than if the subject were an adult.
